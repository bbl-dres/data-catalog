What is a data catalogue?

A metadata catalogue is the common language for an organisation's data. In federal real estate management, different departments and systems often use different terms for the same concepts. This lack of standardisation prevents interoperability and hinders collaboration. The metadata catalogue establishes uniform standards and definitions for all business objects and transparently documents their relationships, responsibilities and usage possibilities. As a central reference, it provides the foundation for interconnected data management within the Federal Administration.

Why is this relevant for us?

The FOBL manages the Confederation's real estate portfolio with thousands of data elements from various specialist applications. Manual documentation of this complex data landscape is no longer manageable. At the same time, regulatory requirements (FADP, OIAF, ISA, FoIA) demand comprehensive evidence of our data processing activities. Without systematic metadata management, compliance risks become incalculable and the once-only principle remains unattainable.

What are the objectives of this metadata catalogue?

  • Three-tier metadata model: Clear separation between business objects, data modelling and technical implementation according to the TOGAF standard
  • Business-friendly interface: Comprehensible presentation without technical complexity, with full integration of Innovator EA in the background
  • Automated processes: API-driven management for import, export and bulk mutations
  • Compliance instrument: Automated evidence for legal requirements at the click of a button
  • National integration: Synchronisation with i14y, opendata.swiss, simap.ch and TERMDAT
  • Data governance: Clearly defined roles and responsibilities for each dataset

Who benefits from a metadata catalogue?

  • Business units: Comprehensible documentation of their business objects and clearly identified contact persons
  • IT architects: Efficient management of data models and standards
  • IT teams: Precise technical specifications for system integrations
  • Compliance: Automated reports for audits and data protection officers
  • Management: Transparent decision-making foundations based on reliable metadata
  • External partners: Standardised access to public real estate and procurement data

Next steps

We will continue to refine the catalogue by reaching out to data owners, updating existing entries and examining integration with other platforms. Over time, we aim to make this tool an indispensable resource for internal and external partners, enabling data-driven innovations across all areas.
