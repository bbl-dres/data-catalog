# The data catalog market in 2026: a Swiss federal lens

The metadata catalog market in 2026 is **mid-consolidation, mid-AI-rebrand, and mid-standards-convergence** — and the choice for a Swiss federal buyer is no longer "pick the best catalog" but "compose two or three layers, anchor them on open standards, and stay portable as the consolidation wave continues." Seven of the most cited independent vendors (Informatica, data.world, Castor, Metaplane, Octopai, Secoda, Numbers Station, Select Star, Manta) were swallowed in 2023–2025 by Salesforce, ServiceNow, Coalesce, Datadog, Cloudera, Atlassian, Alation, Snowflake and IBM respectively. At the same time, the **Iceberg REST catalog** (Apache Polaris, Unity Catalog OSS, Gravitino, Nessie) emerged as the de-facto open layer for table metadata, while **DCAT-AP 3.0** (June 2024) [W3C](https://www.w3.org/news/2024/data-catalog-vocabulary-dcat-version-3-is-a-w3c-recommendation/) and **DCAT-AP CH 3.0.1** (eCH-0200, approved 22 December 2025) re-aligned the Swiss public-sector profile with EU semantics. For BBL, the practical implication is clean: **CKAN-class tooling for DCAT-AP CH publication into opendata.swiss / I14Y, an OSS or commercial catalog for internal discovery, OpenLineage for lineage portability, and Iceberg REST as the lakehouse contract** — with vendor lock-in pushed up to the governance UX layer where it is cheapest to swap.

This report clusters the vendors as the patterns emerged from the research, sizes the market, traces the M&A wave, and ends with explicit recommendations for Swiss federal use.

## How the market actually divides in 2026

The market no longer fits the old "metadata catalog" box. Six clusters emerged from the screening, each with a distinct buying logic and a different relationship to DCAT-AP CH and Swiss residency.

**Enterprise governance suites** (Collibra, Informatica/Salesforce, IBM watsonx.data intelligence, erwin/Quest, Ataccama, Alation) are the heavyweight tier — six- to seven-figure ACVs, six- to twelve-month implementations, full glossary/policy/quality/lineage stacks. They dominate Gartner's new **Magic Quadrant for Data and Analytics Governance Platforms** (inaugural Jan 2025; second edition Jan 2026) and the **Forrester Wave for Data Governance Solutions Q3 2025**, which Forrester explicitly framed as "**the agentic era**." [Forrester](https://www.forrester.com/blogs/the-forrester-wave-data-governance-solutions-q3-2025-shows-that-governance-entered-the-agentic-era/) Collibra (Belgium HQ, Belgian-DPA-approved BCRs, EU residency, ISO 42001, EU AI Pact signatory) [Collibra +2](https://www.collibra.com/) and Ataccama (Prague HQ, Snowflake Ventures-backed Dec 2025) are the strongest sovereignty fits in this tier; [GlobeNewswire](https://www.globenewswire.com/news-release/2025/12/09/3202458/0/en/Ataccama-solidifies-data-trust-leadership-with-investment-from-Snowflake-Ventures.html) Informatica has the most complete capability set but **closed under Salesforce ownership on 18 November 2025 for USD 8 billion**, [Salesforce](https://www.salesforce.com/news/press-releases/2025/11/18/salesforce-completes-acquisition-of-informatica/) [sec](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/0001108524/000110852425000207/ex991-infaclosingpressrele.htm) introducing genuine roadmap uncertainty for non-Salesforce-aligned public buyers.

**Modern SaaS active-metadata catalogs** (Atlan, Alation, the now-acquired Castor/Coalesce-Catalog, Secoda/Atlassian, Select Star/Snowflake, Zeenea/Actian-HCL, DataGalaxy) sit in the USD 50–250k entry band, deploy in 4–8 weeks, and have absorbed most of the AI/MCP innovation. **Atlan was named Leader in Gartner's returned Metadata Management MQ (24 November 2025)** [Yahoo Finance](https://finance.yahoo.com/news/atlan-named-leader-2025-gartner-113500417.html) and Forrester's EDC Wave Q3 2024; [Atlan +2](https://atlan.com/atlan-raises-105m-funding/) its "Iceberg-native metadata lakehouse" [Atlan](https://atlan.com/know/data-catalog/microsoft-purview-and-atlan/) [Yahoo Finance](https://finance.yahoo.com/news/atlan-named-leader-2025-gartner-113500417.html) architecture [Atlan](https://atlan.com/gartner-magic-quadrant-for-metadata-management/) is the most architecturally novel idea in the segment. None of these tools natively speak DCAT-AP — exporters exist via API, but nothing matches the out-of-box conformance of an RDF-native or CKAN-class platform.

**Hyperscaler / cloud-native catalogs** split into two tiers after the 2024–2025 "catalog wars." On top sits a proprietary business/governance layer (Microsoft Purview Unified Catalog + Fabric OneLake Catalog, AWS SageMaker Catalog/DataZone, Google Knowledge Catalog née Dataplex, [Google](https://docs.cloud.google.com/dataplex/docs/transition-to-dataplex-catalog) Snowflake Horizon, Databricks Unity Catalog managed, Oracle OCI Data Catalog, Salesforce Data 360 post-Informatica). Beneath sits an **open Iceberg REST layer** — Apache Polaris (Snowflake-donated, ASF, [Snowflake](https://www.snowflake.com/en/blog/polaris-catalog-open-source/) [Snowflake](https://www.snowflake.com/en/engineering-blog/apache-polaris-iceberg-rest-catalog/) v1.3 Dec 2025), Unity Catalog OSS (Databricks-donated to LF AI & Data, June 2024), [Databricks +2](https://www.databricks.com/blog/data-ai-summit-2024-executive-summary-data-leaders) Apache Gravitino (Datastrato, ASF TLP June 2025), [DEV Community](https://dev.to/datastrato/apache-gravitino-2025-summary-nf8) Project Nessie (Dremio), [E6data](https://www.e6data.com/blog/iceberg-catalogs-2025-emerging-catalogs-modern-metadata-management) [DEV Community](https://dev.to/alexmercedcoder/shades-of-open-source-understanding-the-many-meanings-of-open-35je) AWS S3 Tables (re:Invent 2024). [Amazon UK Press Centre](https://press.aboutamazon.com/2024/12/amazon-s3-expands-capabilities-with-managed-apache-iceberg-tables-for-faster-data-lake-analytics-and-automatic-metadata-generation-to-simplify-data-discovery-and-understanding) [StorageNewsletter](https://www.storagenewsletter.com/2024/12/04/aws-reinvent-amazon-s3-expands-capabilities-with-managed-apache-iceberg-tables-for-faster-data-lake-analytics-and-automatic-metadata-generation/) The strategic meaning is decisive: **table-level operations are now portable** because every serious engine implements the Iceberg REST spec; [Snowflake +2](https://www.snowflake.com/en/blog/introducing-polaris-catalog/) lock-in moves up the stack to glossary, policy, lineage and AI features. [Snowflake](https://www.snowflake.com/en/engineering-blog/apache-polaris-iceberg-rest-catalog/) **Microsoft Purview is the only hyperscaler catalog with a fully resident Swiss data plane** (Switzerland North + Switzerland West Azure regions, [Datacenters](https://www.datacenters.com/news/microsoft-opens-two-cloud-regions-in-switzerland) EU Data Boundary completed Feb 2025, [Microsoft Blogs](https://blogs.microsoft.com/on-the-issues/2025/02/26/microsoft-completes-landmark-eu-data-boundary-offering-enhanced-data-residency-and-transparency/) Sovereign Cloud GA 2025/26); Oracle OCI Data Catalog is also explicitly available in `eu-zurich-1`; [Oracle](https://docs.oracle.com/en-us/iaas/releasenotes/services/data-catalog/) AWS Zurich runs Glue/Lake Formation/SageMaker; Google has europe-west6.

**Open-source catalogs** divide cleanly between three sub-clusters. The "modern enterprise" pair — **DataHub** (Apache 2.0, ~11.7k stars, ~590 contributors, 80+ connectors, Kafka+ES+graph stack, **DataHub Inc. Series B $35 m May 2025**) and **OpenMetadata** (Collate-led, ~9.6k stars, 50+ connectors, simpler MySQL/Postgres+Elasticsearch stack, v1.11.8 Feb 2026) — are the two credible "internal catalog" picks but neither has native DCAT-AP. The "public-sector / open-data" sub-cluster is dominated by **CKAN** (AGPL, stewardship transitioned in 2024 to Link Digital + Datopian, [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/CKAN) v2.11 Aug 2024, **`ckanext-dcat` 2.0+ ships native DCAT-AP v1/v2/v3, DCAT-US v3, HealthDCAT-AP profiles**, and the Swiss `opendata-swiss/*` extensions are actively maintained as of late 2025); [GitHub](https://github.com/opendata-swiss?language=python) **Magda** lost its flagship `data.gov.au` deployment in mid-2025 and should be considered declining; **Piveau** (Fraunhofer FOKUS) powers `data.europa.eu`. The "table-format catalogs" — Polaris, Unity OSS, Gravitino, Nessie — are wrong-layer for DCAT-AP but vital for any future BBL lakehouse. **Apache Atlas** had its first release in over two years (2.4.0, Jan 2025); [Atlan](https://atlan.com/what-is-apache-atlas/) treat it as legacy. **Amundsen is effectively dormant** (Stemma absorbed by Teradata 2023, [Atlan](https://atlan.com/amundsen-vs-datahub/) no roadmap). **Egeria v6.0** (March 2026) [Egeria-project](https://egeria-project.org/introduction/) is worth tracking for federation patterns.

**Active-metadata / observability vendors with catalog overlap** (Monte Carlo, Acceldata, Bigeye, Soda, Sifflet, Datafold, Anomalo, Validio) are observability-first, catalog-second. The most notable 2025 events were **Datadog's acquisition of Metaplane (April 2025)** [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2025/04/23/datadog-acquires-ai-powered-observability-startup-metaplane/) [Yahoo Finance](https://finance.yahoo.com/news/datadog-brings-observability-data-teams-120000765.html) and Acceldata's pivot to "Agentic Data Management." [Acceldata](https://www.acceldata.io/montecarlo-vs-acceldata) For Swiss residency, **Soda (Brussels, Belgium)** and **Sifflet (France)** are the natural EU picks; Acceldata's hybrid/on-prem control plane [Acceldata](https://www.acceldata.io/montecarlo-vs-acceldata) is also relevant. **OpenLineage support is the litmus test** — Marquez is the reference implementation, [The ByteDoodle Blog +3](https://blog.bytedoodle.com/data-lineage-through-openlineage-events/) IBM watsonx.data added native OpenLineage emission in Nov 2025, [IBM](https://www.ibm.com/new/announcements/openlineage-for-a-unified-lineage-view-across-structured-and-unstructured-data-to-enable-explainable-ai) Astronomer/Airflow/dbt/Spark/Flink all emit OpenLineage events, [Ovaledge](https://www.ovaledge.com/blog/data-lineage-tools) [Marquezproject](https://marquezproject.ai/) and Datadog publishes heavily on it. Monte Carlo remains a notable holdout, preferring proprietary integrations.

**Semantic-layer / knowledge-graph catalogs** are a small but architecturally important cluster: **TopQuadrant TopBraid EDG** (the strongest pure SHACL/SKOS/OWL fit for Swiss multilingual taxonomies), **Stardog** (RDF + virtualisation + Voicebox LLM), **data.world** (RDF/SPARQL native, **acquired by ServiceNow May 2025, closed H2 2025**), [Hostnoc +2](https://hostnoc.com/servicenow-to-acquire-dataworld/) Cambridge Semantics Anzo, Franz AllegroGraph. Pure semantic layers (Cube, dbt Semantic Layer / MetricFlow Apache 2.0 from late 2025, AtScale with the new SML spec, Looker LookML) are converging with catalogs through MCP exposure but are not catalogs themselves. The **Open Semantic Interchange (OSI)** spec — released January 2026 with Snowflake, dbt, Cube, AtScale, Databricks and ~40 others — is an early attempt at portability of metric definitions.

**Data-mesh / data-product platforms** moved from peak-hype (2022) to chastened maturity. Pure mesh organizational change is rare; **data-as-a-product contracts and federated catalogs survived**. **Nextdata** (Zhamak Dehghani's company) launched **Nextdata OS on 8 April 2025** with Mars Pet Nutrition as reference customer [GlobeNewswire](https://www.globenewswire.com/news-release/2025/04/08/3057901/0/en/Founded-by-Zhamak-Dehghani-Nextdata-Launches-Nextdata-OS-to-Help-Enterprises-Automate-Data-Management-for-Agents-Analytics-and-Apps.html) [BigDATAwire](https://www.bigdatawire.com/2025/04/16/data-mesh-creator-dehghani-launches-first-product-nextdata-os/) — the first real productisation of mesh, [HPCwire](https://www.hpcwire.com/bigdatawire/2025/04/16/data-mesh-creator-dehghani-launches-first-product-nextdata-os/) but pre-GA. Marketplaces inside Collibra, Atlan, data.world, Starburst Galaxy and **Dawiso** (Czech vendor, multilingual, EU-resident, on-prem capable, materially cheaper than legacy enterprise suites — a credible challenger pick for DACH public sector) are the practical face of mesh in 2026.

## Market sizing, growth and analyst positioning

**Market sizing is fragmented and directional.** Gartner, Forrester and IDC publish little free hard sizing for the catalog/metadata segment specifically. Triangulating across Mordor (USD 3.67 bn 2025 → 9.22 bn 2030, 20.2% CAGR), [Mordor Intelligence](https://www.mordorintelligence.com/industry-reports/data-catalog-market) Fortune Business Insights (USD 1.27 bn 2025 → 4.54 bn 2032, 19.9%), [Fortune Business Insights](https://www.fortunebusinessinsights.com/data-catalog-market-108449) MarketsandMarkets (USD 0.7 bn 2022 → 1.8 bn 2027, 22%), [GlobeNewswire](https://www.globenewswire.com/news-release/2023/01/30/2597584/0/en/Data-Catalog-Market-Size-Hits-1-8-Billion-by-2027-growing-at-a-CAGR-of-22-0-Report-by-MarketsandMarkets.html) Future Market Insights, IMARC and Verified Market Research, the **narrow data-catalog software TAM in 2025 lands plausibly at USD 1.3–3.7 bn growing at ~20% CAGR**. The broader D&A governance + metadata + observability + lineage envelope is meaningfully larger (USD 10–15 bn). Salesforce's framing of a "USD 150 bn+ enterprise data market" in the Informatica deal [Elevatiq](https://www.elevatiq.com/post/salesforce-informatica-acquisition-2025/) includes integration, MDM, quality, and management — not catalog alone. ## Eight trends that actually matter

1. **Active metadata won the architecture but lost the term.** The concept survived (lineage activation, automated stewardship, usage-driven recommendations); [Quest](https://www.quest.com/) the label is fading in favour of "metadata lakehouse" (Atlan), [Yahoo Finance](https://finance.yahoo.com/news/atlan-named-leader-2025-gartner-113500417.html) "data context platform" (DataHub), "data intelligence platform" (Collibra, Databricks) or "control plane" (Alation). Gartner's return to a Metadata Management MQ in Nov 2025 validated the capability without re-anointing the active-metadata wording.

2. **Catalogs are converging with observability, governance, lineage and access control.** Hard evidence: Datadog→Metaplane, [Datadog](https://www.datadoghq.com/) Coalesce→Castor, [Coalesce](https://coalesce.io/) ServiceNow→data.world, Salesforce→Informatica, Cloudera→Octopai, [Arnon,Tadmor-Levi](https://www.arnontl.com/) IBM→Manta [IBM](https://www.ibm.com/) +DataStax, Atlassian→Secoda. Three years from now, "catalog" and "observability" are likely to be features of a single category — Gartner's MQ relaunch is the leading indicator.

3. **AI features split cleanly into "real" and "marketing."** Auto-classification of PII, auto-generation of column descriptions, semantic search and usage-driven enrichment are mature and useful (Atlan, Alation, Collibra Copilot, Stardog Voicebox, Coalesce Catalog, Dawiso). NL→SQL via dedicated agents (Numbers Station/Alation, Cortex Analyst, Databricks Genie) works in narrow domains with evaluation frameworks but hallucinates badly without them. Agentic data discovery is largely roadmap-ware in production as of mid-2026 despite Forrester's "agentic era" branding. MCP (Model Context Protocol) servers exposing catalog metadata to LLM agents shipped in 2025 from Atlan, Alation, Select Star, Datafold, Dawiso and Sifflet [GlobeNewswire +2](https://www.globenewswire.com/) — an early but credible pattern.

4. **Open standards are the real architectural story.** OpenLineage (LF AI & Data graduated, [Debezium](https://debezium.io/) [Lfaidata](https://lfaidata.foundation/) IBM watsonx.data native emission Nov 2025), [Medium](https://medium.com/) DCAT 3 (W3C Recommendation 22 Aug 2024), [W3C](https://www.w3.org/news/2024/data-catalog-vocabulary-dcat-version-3-is-a-w3c-recommendation/) [W3C](https://www.w3.org/TR/vocab-dcat-3/) DCAT-AP 3.0.1 (SEMIC, Oct 2025) and 3.0.2 draft (Jan 2026), DCAT-AP CH 3.0.1 (eCH-0200 approved 22 Dec 2025), [Ech](https://www.ech.ch/) Apache Iceberg REST Catalog, Apache Polaris (TLP-track, [Snowflake](https://www.snowflake.com/en/blog/polaris-catalog-open-source/) v1.3 Dec 2025), [Snowflake](https://www.snowflake.com/en/engineering-blog/apache-polaris-iceberg-rest-catalog/) Unity Catalog OSS, Apache Gravitino (TLP June 2025), [DEV Community +4](https://dev.to/datastrato/apache-gravitino-2025-summary-nf8) the Data Contracts spec / ODCS, and the Open Semantic Interchange spec (Jan 2026) together push the catalog of 2026 toward a federated metadata layer rather than a monolithic SaaS lock-in. For Swiss federal procurement this is an unambiguously favourable shift — open standards de-risk the consolidation wave.

5. **Knowledge graphs are winning the architecture and losing the terminology war.** Pure RDF/SPARQL/SHACL catalogs (TopBraid EDG, Stardog, data.world, AllegroGraph) remain a minority of deployments but have the strongest defensible architecture for multilingual public-sector use because SKOS is the standard for multilingual controlled vocabularies — a direct match for Swiss DE/FR/IT/RM reality. Atlan, Collibra, Alation and DataHub all use graph storage internally [data.world](https://data.world/) and increasingly call it a "knowledge graph" — convergent terminology, divergent semantic depth.

6. **Data mesh borrowed its ideas, not its org chart.** Precisely's 2024 Trends survey shows mesh/fabric influence on data programs at 18% (up from 13% in 2023) [Precisely](https://www.precisely.com/) — modest growth, not breakout. The pattern that survived is data-product contracts + domain ownership inside a federated catalog backbone. Collibra Data Marketplace, [PR Newswire](https://www.prnewswire.com/) Atlan Data Products, Starburst Galaxy and Dawiso are the practical face of this; Nextdata OS (April 2025) is the first real productisation [Yahoo Finance +2](https://finance.yahoo.com/) but not yet GA.

7. **OSS wins the bottom of the market, commercial wins the regulated top.** DataHub, OpenMetadata, CKAN, Unity Catalog OSS, Polaris and Gravitino dominate lakehouse-native shops and modern-stack mid-market; Collibra, Informatica, IBM and Ataccama still dominate regulated industries and EU public sector for certified support, audit warranties and DACH presence. A two-tier market is the durable outcome.

8. **EU regulation is the forcing function for AI/data governance.** The EU AI Act (in force 1 Aug 2024; high-risk obligations through Aug 2026/27), the EU Data Act (Reg. 2023/2854, [Coalesce](https://coalesce.io/) applicable since 12 September 2025, [Coalesce +2](https://coalesce.io/) full cloud-switching obligations from 12 Jan 2027), [Eubelius](https://www.eubelius.com/) the Data Governance Act (applicable 24 Sep 2023), [Clifford Chance](https://www.cliffordchance.com/) [European Commission](https://ec.europa.eu/) the High-Value Datasets IR (applies 9 June 2024, six categories, [data.europa.eu](https://data.europa.eu/) mandatory CC0/CC-BY 4.0), the EHDS (Reg. 2025/327, in force 26 Mar 2025) [Public](https://www.publicnow.com/) and the proposed EU Digital Omnibus (late 2025/early 2026) collectively make AI-BoM, model registries, lineage to model and audit-grade evidence mandatory. Switzerland is not in the EU but the Federal Council decided in Feb 2025 to align via a sectoral approach plus the Council of Europe AI Convention signed in March 2024. Catalog choice must demonstrably support these requirements regardless of Swiss non-membership.

## The Swiss federal lens — what this all means for BBL

The Swiss federal context narrows the field sharply. Five constraints dominate.

1. **Legal and procurement constraints.** The revDSG / nFADP (in force 1 Sep 2023) [Datenrecht](https://datenrecht.ch/) is GDPR-aligned but stricter on cross-border transparency — disclosing the specific countries of processing and safeguards. [ZAZOON](https://zazoon.ch/) EMBAG (in force 1 Jan 2024) [Datenrecht](https://datenrecht.ch/) anchors three principles relevant to BBL: Open Government Data by default (Art. 10), Open Source Software by default (Art. 9), [Parldigi](https://parldigi.ch/) and a legal basis for I14Y as the federal interoperability platform. The DigiV ordinance prescribes "digital first" workflows. The Public Clouds Bund WTO-20007 framework (signed Sep 2022 with AWS, Microsoft, IBM, Oracle, Alibaba, CHF 110 m ceiling) [Netzwoche](https://www.netzwoche.ch/) was extended to 2031, [Admin +2](https://www.admin.ch/) and contracts were published under BGÖ on 21 October 2025. [Steiger Legal](https://steigerlegal.ch/) [Bundeskanzlei](https://www.bk.admin.ch/) The Swiss Government Cloud (SGC) programme (CHF 246.9 m commitment credit approved Dec 2024, run by BIT, [www.aktuellenews.ch](https://www.aktuellenews.ch/) [Admin](https://www.admin.ch/) three tiers: Private Cloud Bund / Public Cloud Switzerland / Public Cloud) [BIT](https://www.bit.admin.ch/) goes live 2026 and reaches productive migration 2027–2032. [BIT +2](https://www.bit.admin.ch/) Switzerland is described in EU press as the first European nation (ex-Monaco) to launch a state sovereign cloud. [GettoText](https://gettotext.com/)

2. **Standards and platforms constraints.** DCAT-AP CH v3.0.1 (eCH-0200, approved 22 Dec 2025) is the binding profile, aligned to DCAT-AP 3.0 [Ech](https://www.ech.ch/) and validated via SHACL shapes maintained on GitHub (factsmission/dcat-ap-ch-shacl, dcat-ap.ch). The companion eCH-0285 governs controlled vocabularies. [Dcat-ap](https://dcat-ap.ch/) opendata.swiss runs on CKAN (Liip-built since 2013, [Liip](https://www.liip.ch/) decoupled Nest.js frontend since 2024 refactor, [Liip](https://www.liip.ch/) hosted by Datopian) [Liip](https://www.liip.ch/) using ckanext-dcat, ckanext-harvest, ckanext-geocat [Liip](https://www.liip.ch/) and the opendata-swiss/* extensions. I14Y (i14y.admin.ch) is [GitHub](https://github.com/opendata-swiss?language=python) a custom platform — not CKAN — operated by the BFS Interoperabilitätsstelle and developed in an Agile Release Train; it federates DCAT-AP CH metadata in real time with geocat.ch and other federal sources, [Bundesamt für Statistik](https://www.bfs.admin.ch/) and goes operational at end-2026. [I14y-ch](https://www.i14y.admin.ch/) geocat.ch runs GeoNetwork (ISO-19139_che, [Liip](https://www.liip.ch/) OGC CSW [GitHub](https://github.com/) 2.0.2, swisstopo/KOGIS). [Geocat](https://www.geocat.ch/) [geocat.ch](https://www.geocat.ch/) LINDAS (lindas.admin.ch) is the federal Linked Data SPARQL endpoint, [Admin](https://lindas.admin.ch/) operated under the Federal Archives, [Admin](https://www.bar.admin.ch/) built largely by Zazuko. NaDB (Nationale Datenbewirtschaftung, [GitHub](https://github.com/) BFS-led, extended to end-2026) implements the once-only principle. [Bundesamt für Statistik](https://www.bfs.admin.ch/) [Ech](https://www.ech.ch/)

3. **Language constraints.** DE/FR/IT (and where realistic RM) UI plus multilingual metadata content are non-negotiable. SKOS-native catalogs and CKAN with ckanext-scheming are the strongest fits; most US SaaS catalogs handle bilingual metadata only through custom configuration.

4. **Residency reality.** Only Microsoft Azure (Switzerland North/West, full Fabric/Purview/Power BI in-region, EU Data Boundary completed Feb 2025, Sovereign Cloud GA 2025/26), AWS (Zurich since Nov 2022, 173+ services, [AWS](https://aws.amazon.com/) Glue/Lake Formation/SageMaker available, AWS European Sovereign Cloud GA late 2025 [Futurum Group](https://futurumgroup.com/) in Brandenburg), Google Cloud (europe-west6 Zurich, full Knowledge Catalog/BigQuery) and Oracle (eu-zurich-1, OCI Data Catalog explicitly listed, [Oracle](https://docs.oracle.com/en-us/iaas/releasenotes/services/data-catalog/) EU Sovereign Cloud since June 2023) [Cloudvisor](https://cloudvisor.co/) have physical Swiss regions with full catalog data planes. Snowflake and Databricks inherit residency from underlying hyperscalers (both available on AWS Zurich and Azure Switzerland North/West). Salesforce/Informatica, Confluent and most US SaaS catalogs have no Swiss region. The control plane caveat applies to all of them.

5. **DCAT-AP CH compatibility ranking.** This is the single sharpest filter. CKAN with ckanext-dcat 2.0+ ships native DCAT-AP v1/v2/v3 profiles, [CKAN](https://ckan.org/) DCAT-AP CH-specific extensions are actively maintained by opendata-swiss, and the platform is AGPL-friendly to EMBAG Art. 9. Piveau (Fraunhofer FOKUS) is DCAT-AP-3-native and powers data.europa.eu. data.world / Stardog / TopBraid EDG / AllegroGraph / Cambridge Semantics are RDF/SPARQL/SHACL-native and can model DCAT-AP CH directly. Collibra, Informatica, IBM support DCAT export through connectors but not first-class. Atlan, Alation, Coalesce Catalog, Secoda, Select Star, Dawiso, Castor generally lack native DCAT-AP exporters; custom mapping via API/SDK is required. DataHub, OpenMetadata, ODD Platform, Apache Atlas, Truedat — none have native DCAT-AP support. Acceldata, Monte Carlo, Sifflet, Bigeye, Anomalo, Soda — observability tools have no DCAT concept.

## Vendor shortlist for BBL

Three tiers emerge from the screening, each with distinct scope.

### Tier A — strongly recommended for the public-facing DCAT-AP CH layer

The recommendation is unambiguous. **CKAN** remains the default for any DCAT-AP CH publication portal federating with opendata.swiss and I14Y. Its DCAT-AP support is the deepest in the market, the Swiss `opendata-swiss/*` extensions are actively maintained, AGPL aligns with EMBAG Art. 9, and Swiss integrators (Liip, Datopian, Begasoft, Adfinis, Puzzle ITC) have deep deployment experience. Its frontend is dated (Bootstrap 3, jQuery, Jinja); the Liip 2024 decoupled-frontend pattern (Nest.js front, CKAN back) is the credible workaround. [Liip](https://www.liip.ch/) **Piveau** is a viable alternative if the team has Vert.x/RDF skills. **GeoNetwork** is the natural choice for any geospatial subset, harvested into the broader catalog. Linked Data publication of high-value reference data through **LINDAS** (Zazuko-operated) is the federal-grade pattern for stable URIs and SPARQL endpoints.

### Tier B — strong candidates for an internal enterprise catalog (governance / discovery / lineage)

For internal BBL data discovery — distinct from the public DCAT-AP CH publication layer — the strongest candidates are:

## Recommended architecture pattern

Compose three layers rather than picking one tool.

**Layer 1 — public open-data publication.** CKAN (with `opendata-swiss/*` extensions) or Piveau as the DCAT-AP CH 3.0.1 publication endpoint, harvested by opendata.swiss and federated with I14Y. Geo subset via geocat.ch (GeoNetwork, ISO-19139_che). High-value reference data published as Linked Data through LINDAS (Zazuko / Apache Jena Fuseki). Validate against `factsmission/dcat-ap-ch-shacl`. SEO via schema.org/Dataset JSON-LD (already in `ckanext-dcat`). [Liip](https://www.liip.ch/) [CKAN](https://ckan.org/)

**Layer 2 — internal data intelligence.** A modern enterprise catalog for BBL analysts and engineers. First choice: **Microsoft Purview** if the BBL estate is Microsoft-heavy (likely, given Public Clouds Bund). Open-source alternative: **OpenMetadata** with a custom DCAT-AP CH export profile, deployed on SGC Private Cloud. EU-vendor alternative: **Zeenea, Collibra, Dawiso or Ataccama** for stronger sovereignty/multilingual posture. Add **Solidatus** (Microsoft's named lineage partner) if Purview is the chosen catalog and advanced lineage is needed.

**Layer 3 — open standards substrate.** OpenLineage events from all pipelines (Airflow, dbt, Spark, [Atlan](https://atlan.com/) Flink) into a Marquez or catalog-native backend. [Ovaledge](https://www.ovaledge.com/blog/data-lineage-tools) Iceberg REST Catalog (Polaris or Unity Catalog OSS) for any analytical lakehouse on SGC, keeping table-level operations portable across engines. Data contracts (ODCS / datacontract.com) for producer-consumer governance. SKOS-controlled vocabularies for multilingual taxonomies.

This pattern keeps the public-facing DCAT-AP CH layer fully sovereign and standards-conformant, isolates the enterprise catalog choice as the most reversible decision (the consolidation wave makes lock-in here costly), and uses open standards as the contract between layers so that any single vendor swap at next refresh is a configuration change rather than a re-platforming.

## Closing perspective and key uncertainties

The data catalog market in 2026 is no longer a vendor-selection problem; it is a standards-and-architecture problem with vendor choices that should be re-evaluated every three to five years. The Swiss federal context tilts the answer hard toward open standards and EU/CH-domiciled tooling, with hyperscaler catalogs (Microsoft Purview most credibly) acceptable where Swiss residency and Public Clouds Bund framework coverage exist. CKAN's continued dominance for DCAT-AP CH publication is one of the few stable certainties; everything else is in motion.

Several uncertainties deserve flagging. DCAT-AP CH 3.0.1 is fresh (approved 22 Dec 2025) and tooling will iterate through 2026 — verify SHACL shape conformance before relying on validators. I14Y remains in project phase until end-2026; [I14y-ch](https://www.i14y.admin.ch/) lock in a contact at the BFS Interoperabilitätsstelle before architecting against specific features. SGC is approved but not productive until 2027; plan a dual-track today (BIT private cloud) → SGC migration. Salesforce-Informatica integration will only stabilise through 2026 — Swiss residency continuity and DCAT roadmap are open questions. Gaia-X has narrowed its ambition to a labelling/compliance framework rather than a runtime; [all-about-industries](https://www.all-about-industries.com/) treat it as an optional label, not architectural commitment, [Cloudvisor](https://cloudvisor.co/) and prefer the more concrete Catena-X / EDC / IDSA Dataspace Protocol references where data-space patterns are needed. EU Data Act model contractual terms missed their September 2025 deadline; [Eubelius](https://www.eubelius.com/) any catalog/cloud contract written today should anticipate retroactive Chapter IV obligations from 12 September 2027. [Eubelius](https://www.eubelius.com/) And finally, "agentic" claims warrant POC verification — demand evaluation frameworks, MCP exposure and bidirectional metadata writes as the three concrete tests of real agentic capability versus relabelled AI.

The composable, standards-anchored architecture recommended above is robust to all of these uncertainties. It is also, not coincidentally, the architecture the EU itself adopts for `data.europa.eu` — and the one most consistent with EMBAG's open-source-by-default principle. For BBL, that convergence is the strongest signal that the choice is sound.
